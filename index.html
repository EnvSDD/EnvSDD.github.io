EnvSDD: Benchmarking Environmental Sound Deepfake Detection

EnvSDD Organization

Abstract

Advanced audio generation systems now create very realistic soundscapes that can enhance virtual reality and media production, but also pose potential risks for misuse. Several studies have examined deepfakes in speech or singing voice. However, environmental sounds have different characteristics, which may make methods for speech and singing less effective. In addition, existing datasets for environmental sound deepfake detection are limited in scale and audio types. To address this gap, we introduce EnvSDD, the first large-scale curated dataset designed for this task, consisting of 45.25 hours of real and 316.74 hours of fake audio. The test set includes diverse conditions to evaluate performance across various scenarios, such as unseen audio generation models and unseen datasets. Results on EnvSDD show that our proposed system, based on a pre-trained audio foundation model, outperforms the state-of-the-art systems from speech and singing deepfake detection domains.
